Amy Ching-Yan Lam is an artist and writer.

Exhibitions, performances, and screenings have been presented at Seoul MediaCity Biennale, South Korea; Eastside Projects, UK; SFU Galleries, Richmond Art Gallery, the Western Front, Centre Clark, Truck Gallery, aka artist-run, Art Gallery of Ontario, and many others in Canada.

She is the author of Baby Book (2023), a finalist for the Governor General’s Literary Awards in Poetry; Looty Goes to Heaven (2022); and Property Journal (2024).

She has been artist-in-residence at Delfina Foundation, Macdowell, International Studio & Curatorial Program, Art Gallery of Ontario, Toronto Biennial of Art, Banff Centre for the Arts, Hotel MariaKapel, and the Department of Safety, amongst others.

From 2006–2020, she was part of the performance art duo Life of a Craphead. Life of a Craphead was involved with numerous DIY projects in Toronto, including the performance and music venue Double Double Land.

Amy has been an organizer with Friends of Chinatown Toronto and currently with Writers Against the War on Gaza, Toronto chapter.

She was born in Hong Kong and lives in Tkaronto/Toronto.

With thanks to the Canada Council for the Arts, Toronto Arts Council, and Ontario Arts Council.
